# Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?
DriverPass provides a better way for student drivers to study for their driving exams, schedule appointments to practice for their practical driving tests. DriverPass wanted to create a website that allows customers to create an account, purchase a study package, study and take exams online, and schedule appointments with drivers to practice. Employees of DriverPass also need the ability to purchase packages for customers and schedule their appointments.

# How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?
I felt that I was able to understand the user's needs through technical requirements and interview. I created use cases and diagrams to fit these use cases, which allowed me to see the user's needs. I then added the needs into the website. The users are obviously the ones that use the site the most, so their needs must be at the forefront. 

# How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?
Thinking in terms of functional and non-functional requirements was very helpful for software design. I also felt that creating use case, UML, and Gantt diagrams was very helpful for visualization and giving others a better understanding of what the application should do.
